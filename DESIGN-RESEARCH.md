# Rothenbury Group — Institutional Holding Co. Design Research

> **Cross-brand references stripped per 2026-04-28 directive.** This research note pre-dates the decouple pass; "subsidiary logo grid" patterns referenced below are not implemented on the live site — operating-brand identities are not surfaced publicly. See `DECOUPLE-COMPLETE.md`.


Reference set analyzed: Brookfield Corporation (bn.brookfield.com), Brookfield Asset Management (brookfield.com), Apollo Global Management, Blackstone, Berkshire Hathaway (anti-pattern), Tricon Residential (real-estate adjacency), Fairfax Financial Holdings (closest Canadian analog). Onex, KKR, Rothschild & Co, Power Corporation, and Carlyle either rendered client-side-only or returned 403 to scripted access — patterns from those names are well-represented in the seven that did resolve.

---

## Top patterns to copy (ranked by relevance to Rothenbury)

1. **Fairfax Financial pattern (highest fit).** Headline = literal entity definition. "Canadian holding company focused on building long term shareholder value." Big-number trust block (total assets, gross premiums, CAGR since founding year). Subsidiary logo grid. Sans-serif, blue + white, minimal photography. This is the template — Rothenbury is a Canadian parent holding co; Fairfax is a Canadian parent holding co. Copy the structure.
2. **Brookfield Corp.** Hero is a single declarative sentence ("We are a leading global investment firm focused on building long-term wealth..."). Single primary CTA ("Invest with Us"). Four-card business segment grid with ownership percentages. Stock tickers (NYSE/TSX) shown in header — institutional signal Rothenbury can mimic with "Private — Toronto, Canada" or jurisdictional badges instead.
3. **Blackstone hero stat.** "$1.3T Assets Under Management" + "~12,500 real estate assets and 250+ portfolio companies" appears immediately under the hero. Even without an AUM figure, Rothenbury can use: "13 operating companies. 2 jurisdictions. 1 mission."
4. **Brookfield Asset Mgmt headline ("Own What's Next").** Three-word ownership claim + one-sentence amplifier. This is the move when you don't want to lead with a definition. Optional alternate for Rothenbury: "Building Enduring Companies."
5. **Apollo case-study cards.** Photo-forward portfolio cards with category label, transformation headline, short copy. Use this for the Northstone + 7 brands page — not for the homepage grid (logo grid is faster to scan).

## Headline structures that work for holding companies

- **Definitional:** "Canadian holding company focused on [outcome]." (Fairfax)
- **Ownership-stance:** "Own What's Next." (Brookfield AM)
- **Long-horizon:** "We are a leading global investment firm focused on building long-term wealth..." (Brookfield Corp)
- **Mission-as-product:** "Creating New & Better Solutions for the Modern World." (Apollo) — softer, less recommended for a parent.
- **AVOID interrogative or playful:** "What if..." reads consumer/agency, not institutional.

Recommended Rothenbury hero copy (pick one):
- "Rothenbury Group. A Canadian holding company building enduring operating businesses across real estate, services, and technology."
- "Building enduring companies. From Toronto and Panama City."

CTA pattern across the segment is one primary link, low-pressure verb: "Learn More," "About Us," "Our Portfolio," "Invest with Us." Never "Get Started," "Book a Call," "Sign Up."

## Color palette — confirmed: deep navy / charcoal / off-white dominates

The hypothesis holds. Across the seven sites:

- **Brookfield Corp:** black text on white, light-blue (#CFE6F5) accent, dark-gray secondary text.
- **Brookfield AM:** white/off-white, deep navy/charcoal text, subtle grays (#F0–#F8 range).
- **Blackstone:** black/dark gray on white, near-zero accent color.
- **Apollo:** outlier — uses a green accent + tan/grey neutrals. Distinctive but harder to execute well.
- **Tricon:** navy primary + teal accent on white/gray.
- **Fairfax:** blue + white, almost monochrome.
- **Berkshire:** pure black-on-white (anti-pattern).

**Recommended Rothenbury palette:**
- Primary text/brand: deep navy `#0B1F3A` or near-black `#111418`
- Background: off-white `#FAFAF7` or pure white `#FFFFFF`
- Secondary text: warm gray `#6B7280`
- Single accent (used sparingly for links/CTAs): muted gold `#B8935A` or institutional blue `#1E3A8A`
- Dividers/cards: `#E5E7EB`

A single warm-metal accent (gold, bronze) is the most differentiated move — it reads "establishment / family office" without going kitschy. Tricon's teal and Apollo's green are riskier choices.

## Typography norms — split, but trending toward a serif/sans pairing for the institutional tier

What I observed:
- **All-sans (modern corporate):** Brookfield, Blackstone, Apollo, Fairfax, Tricon. Geometric or neo-grotesque headline, humanist sans body.
- **Serif headline + sans body (heritage / family-office tier):** This is where Rothschild & Co, JPMorgan Private Bank, and most family offices live. The reference set leans sans because the firms are publicly traded operating businesses — Rothenbury is a private parent holding co, which is closer to family-office territory.

**Recommended Rothenbury typography:**
- Display/headline: a refined serif — `GT Sectra`, `Canela`, `Tiempos Headline`, or free alternative `Source Serif 4` / `Cormorant Garamond`.
- Body/UI: a clean neo-grotesque — `Söhne`, `Inter`, `Untitled Sans`, or free `Inter` / `IBM Plex Sans`.
- This serif-display + sans-body pairing signals "private capital / family office" rather than "publicly traded operator." It's the right tonal cue for a top-of-stack parent.

Avoid: Times New Roman, Garamond default, Playfair Display (overused), Montserrat (consumer / startup), Poppins (consumer).

## Imagery — the institutional set leans architectural and abstract; portraits are rare

Observed:
- **Brookfield AM:** hydro dams, data centers, fiber optics, cargo, abstract upward cityscapes.
- **Blackstone:** real estate exteriors, infrastructure photography, branded carousel art.
- **Apollo:** glass office buildings (curved/layered), abstract conceptual visuals, portfolio company exteriors. **No executive portraits.**
- **Brookfield Corp:** abstract geometric textures, SVG line icons. **No photography of people.**
- **Fairfax:** logos, report mockups, near-zero photography.
- **Tricon (anti-reference for Rothenbury):** warm lifestyle photos of families. This is consumer real-estate, not parent holding co. **Do not copy.**
- **Berkshire:** no imagery at all.

**Imagery direction for Rothenbury:**
- Architectural and infrastructural, shot tight and abstracted. Avoid stock-y boardrooms and handshakes.
- Portraits only on Leadership page, never on home; black-and-white or cool-toned color, environmental (subject in office context, not headshot floating on white).
- Abstract textural backgrounds (concrete, brushed metal, frosted glass) for section dividers.

**Unsplash search keywords to feed into the build:**
- `toronto skyline aerial`, `toronto financial district`, `bay street toronto`
- `panama city skyline`, `panama city architecture night`
- `modern corporate architecture`, `glass facade abstract`, `concrete architecture minimal`
- `boardroom dark`, `executive portrait environmental black and white`
- `aerial city dusk`, `infrastructure abstract`, `brushed metal texture`
- AVOID: `business meeting`, `team handshake`, `happy family`, `realtor`, `stock market chart`.

## Navigation — 5 to 8 top-level items, mega-menu only when justified

- Brookfield Corp: 8 items, mega-menu (because it's a public-co IR site).
- Apollo: 7 items, deep mega-menu.
- Blackstone: 5 items, multi-tier dropdowns.
- Fairfax: 9 items, simple top-bar (no mega-menu).
- Berkshire: vertical bullet list (anti-pattern).

**Recommended Rothenbury nav (5 items, simple dropdowns only where needed):**
`About` · `Portfolio` · `Leadership` · `News` · `Contact`
Optional 6th: `Responsibility` (for the Nathan Levinson Charity tie-in). No mega-menu — Rothenbury doesn't have the surface area to justify one and a simple bar reads more confident.

## Footer — what the institutional segment puts there

Common elements across all seven:
- Subsidiary / brand link list (Brookfield, Fairfax, Blackstone all do this — strong fit for Rothenbury's 7 brands)
- Investor/IR contact block with named human + email + phone
- Social links (LinkedIn primary, X/Twitter secondary; rarely Instagram or TikTok at this tier)
- Legal stack: Privacy, Terms, Cookies, Accessibility, Modern Slavery Statement, Sitemap
- Regional offices with addresses
- Copyright with founding-year-to-current range (Berkshire: "1977–2026" — heritage signal)

For Rothenbury specifically: include both the Toronto and Panama City office addresses, link out to Northstone Holdings + all 7 operating brands, and put `info@rothenburygroup.com` (or whichever address Zak provides) with a named contact when leadership info comes in.

## Trust signals when you can't lead with AUM

Rothenbury is private and won't publish portfolio AUM. Substitutes proven by the reference set:

- **Founding year** (Berkshire: 1977; Fairfax: 1985 → "18.9% CAGR since 1985")
- **Geographic reach** ("2 countries. 13 operating companies.")
- **Operational scale** (Blackstone: "12,500 real estate assets and 250+ portfolio companies")
- **Subsidiary logo grid** (Fairfax, Brookfield) — the brands themselves are the proof
- **Press/media mentions** strip (when available)
- **Regulatory/legal credentials** (incorporation jurisdiction, registered office)

Do NOT use: client testimonials, star ratings, BBB badges (Tricon does this; it reads consumer), case-study video thumbnails with play-counts.

## What to AVOID (looks dated, cheesy, or off-brand for institutional)

- Stock photos of diverse smiling teams in glass conference rooms
- Handshake imagery, gear/cog icons, "globe with network lines" hero graphics
- Gradient hero backgrounds with neon accents (SaaS-coded)
- Animated counters that count up from zero (consumer agency tic)
- Carousels with marketing taglines like "Innovation. Excellence. Trust."
- Chatbots, "Schedule a Demo," "Get a Free Quote" CTAs
- Playfair Display + Montserrat (the default agency template)
- Emoji in body copy, exclamation points, sentence-case CTA buttons with action verbs
- Three-color accent palettes; pick one accent and hold the line
- Hero video of city timelapse with swelling music — it's been done by every PE firm since 2015 and now reads stale
- Testimonials section on homepage (this is the single fastest way to look mid-market)

## Build priorities for the Rothenbury site

1. Hero: white background, deep navy serif headline (one definitional sentence), single low-pressure CTA to `/portfolio`.
2. Stat row directly under hero: 3 numbers (operating companies, jurisdictions, founding year or AUM-equivalent).
3. Portfolio section: subsidiary logo grid (Northstone + 7 brands), each tile linking to the brand site.
4. About section: short paragraph, leadership teaser, link to full Leadership page.
5. News/insights: 3 most recent items, card layout.
6. Footer: dense, multi-column, with addresses + subsidiary links + legal stack + named contact.

Reference Fairfax for structure, Brookfield for tone, Apollo for portfolio card detail, Blackstone for stat-block treatment. Do not reference Tricon's homepage for anything except the navy/teal palette decision (which we are not copying — go warm-metal accent instead).
